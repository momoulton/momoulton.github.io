The World of Dorothy L. Sayers: a Portrait of an Artist

About this project

This site explores the life of novelist, poet, and translator Dorothy L. Sayers (1893-1957). It emphasizes context: social context, geographical context, literary context, historical context. No artist is an island; by providing visual means to represent the contexts in which a given artist lived and work, we can explore the connections that shaped a body of work.

This is a work in progress. The underlying data could always be made more complete. While the map treats Sayers' whole life, the social network graph covers only her first thirty years, roughly speaking. Note, in particular, that I don't have accurate dates for when many of Sayers' relationships ended, and have used her death year (1957) as a default, causing many relationships to seem more long-lived than they actually were. If you have contributions or suggestions, please get in touch. My full contact information can be found on my main site.

Technical notes

The social network animation was built using the force-directed graph in the D3.js library. It simulates the interactions between charged particles. Drag nodes around to rearrange the graph and see the relationships between individuals from different perspectives. The slider is based on the NoUiSlider library.

The map is built using the Google Maps API and is fully zoomable.
